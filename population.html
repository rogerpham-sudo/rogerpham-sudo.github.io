Population Change

Populations and demographics are some of the biggest factors when it comes to housing affordability. One biased claim is that the large influx of overseas immigrants has decreased the availability of housing, this idea has been spread primarily by right-wing supporters and people who dislike immigrants to push their agenda.

Figure 4. Estimated Resident Population vs Median Established House Prices

The Australian Government’s estimate for the population of Australia on the right, and median house prices for all of Australia on the left, over time.

Figure 5. Net Overseas Migration vs Median Established House Prices

The overseas arrivals minus the overseas departures on the right (With a moving average of period 4 in a darker orange), and median house prices for all of Australia on the left, over time.

Both median house prices and population in Figure 4 follow a linear trend, except for house prices which spiked in after 2020 as seen in previous figures due to COVID. A cause for the housing crisis that is often thrown around, especially by conservative parties, is the large number of immigrants in Australia decreasing the availability of housing. Figure 4 shows that before 2021, house prices have matched population growth pretty well. Figure 5 shows that overseas migration has almost no correlation with house prices. The period between 2012 and 2020, where migrations stayed almost constant, still showed a steady increase in median house prices. All this data pretty clearly contradicts any theory that immigrants are to blame.
