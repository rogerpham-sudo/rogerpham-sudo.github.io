Prices grow steadily and linearly from 2003 to 2020, except for Sydney, Canberra and Melbourne which are consistently more expensive and less predictable compared to the other regions. Then COVID hit, as indicated by the dashed red line, raising the demand for housing (especially with less people per household) and median prices at an unprecedented rate. This has led to a larger separation in median price between the most and least populated regions as shown by the red arrow. A notable drop between 2008-2010 was caused by the Great Recession and the resulting economic downturn and housing market crash.
